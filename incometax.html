Tax Filing FAQs

For most individual taxpayers, the deadline is July 31st each year. However, for businesses requiring tax audit, the deadline is typically September 30th. There may be extensions announced by the government. We recommend filing early to avoid last-minute rush and potential penalties.

The correct ITR form depends on your income sources:
  • ITR-1: Salary/Pension income up to ₹50 lakh
  • ITR-2: Capital gains or multiple house properties
  • ITR-3: For business income
  • ITR-4: Presumptive taxation (U/s 44AD/ADA)
    • Our experts will determine the correct form based on your specific situation.

If you miss the deadline, you can still file a belated return by December 31st (or until assessment year ends) with penalties:
  • ₹5,000 if filed by December 31st
  • ₹10,000 if filed after December 31st
  • ₹1,000 penalty if total income ≤ ₹5 lakh
We can help minimize penalties and file your late return properly.

You should maintain tax records for at least 6 years from the end of the relevant assessment year. For example, keep FY 2022-23 (AY 2023-24) records until March 31, 2030. This is important in case of any tax notices or audits. We provide secure digital storage for all documents we process.

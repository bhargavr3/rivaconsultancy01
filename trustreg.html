Trust Registration FAQs

Public trusts are created for charitable purposes benefiting the public at large (education, healthcare, poverty relief etc.) and are registered under state Public Trust Acts. Private trusts are created for benefit of specific individuals/families (wealth management, estate planning) and are governed by Indian Trusts Act 1882. The registration process and compliance requirements differ significantly.

The key documents required are:
  • Trust deed on stamp paper (value varies by state)
  • ID proof of settlor and trustees
  • Address proof of registered office
  • No objection certificate from property owner (if applicable)
  • Passport photos of trustees
  • PAN card application
  • Details of trust objectives and activities

The time required depends on the type of trust and state regulations:
  • Private trust registration: 7-10 working days
  • Public charitable trust: 15-30 working days
  • 12A/80G certification: Additional 2-3 months
  • FCRA registration: 3-6 months
We can expedite the process for urgent requirements.

Registered trusts can avail significant tax benefits:
  • 12A Registration: Exempts trust income from tax
  • 80G Certificate: Donors get 50% deduction on donations
  • Exemption from capital gains tax
  • No gift tax on donations received
  • Lower TDS rates on certain payments
These benefits make trust registration highly advantageous for charitable organizations.

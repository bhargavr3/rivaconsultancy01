SIP & Mutual Fund FAQs

You can start a SIP with as little as ₹500 per month for most mutual fund schemes. Some funds may have slightly higher minimums. The amount can be increased later as your financial capacity grows. We help you determine the right SIP amount based on your goals and income.

Selecting the right funds depends on multiple factors including your financial goals, investment horizon, risk tolerance, and tax situation. Our advisors analyze these parameters along with fund performance, expense ratios, and portfolio composition to recommend the most suitable funds for your portfolio.

SIP investments in Equity Linked Savings Schemes (ELSS) qualify for tax deduction under Section 80C up to ₹1.5 lakh per year. However, capital gains from mutual funds are taxable based on holding period and fund type. Short-term gains (held <1 year for equity funds) are taxed at 15%, while long-term gains (≥1 year) over ₹1 lakh are taxed at 10%. Debt funds held ≥3 years are taxed at 20% with indexation benefit.

Yes, SIPs offer complete flexibility. You can pause, increase, decrease, or stop your SIP at any time without any penalty. Some funds may require you to complete a minimum number of installments (typically 6-12), but you can exit anytime after that. We recommend consulting with our advisors before making changes to ensure it aligns with your financial plan.

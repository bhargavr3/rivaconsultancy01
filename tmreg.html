Trademark FAQs

Trademark registration provides legal protection for your brand by:
  • Preventing others from using similar marks
  • Establishing your exclusive rights nationwide
  • Adding value to your business assets
  • Making it easier to take legal action against infringement
  • Building customer trust and brand recognition

The trademark registration process typically takes 12-24 months in India if there are no objections or oppositions. The timeline includes:
  • 1-3 days for trademark search and application preparation
  • 1-2 days for filing (priority date established)
  • 12-18 months for examination and publication
  • 4 months opposition period (if no opposition)
  • 1-2 months for registration certificate issuance

A registered trademark is valid for 10 years from the date of application. You can renew it indefinitely every 10 years by paying the renewal fee. There is no limit to how many times you can renew a trademark, as long as you continue using it and file renewal applications on time.

If there's an objection (from the examiner) or opposition (from a third party), our team will:
  1. Analyze the objection/opposition notice carefully
  2. Prepare a strong response with legal arguments
  3. Submit all required documents and evidence
  4. Represent you in hearings if needed
  5. Work to resolve the matter favorably
Most objections can be overcome with proper response and documentation.

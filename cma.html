CMA Report FAQs

A CMA (Credit Monitoring Arrangement) report is a detailed financial analysis document required by banks when applying for business loans. It includes projected financial statements, fund flow, working capital assessment and repayment capacity evaluation. Banks use this to assess your loan repayment capability before sanctioning credit facilities.

Typically you need to provide:
  • Last 3 years financial statements (balance sheet, P&L)
  • Income tax returns
  • Project details and cost estimates
  • Existing loan details (if any)
  • Bank statements
  • Business plan (for new ventures)

The time required depends on the complexity of your business and loan requirements. Typically:
  • Simple CMA for working capital: 3-5 working days
  • Detailed CMA for term loans: 5-7 working days
  • Complex projects/new ventures: 7-10 working days
